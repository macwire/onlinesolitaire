How to Play Klondike Solitaire: A Complete Beginner's Guide

Soliatre Editorial Team·5 min read

Klondike solitaire is the version that made the game a household name. For millions of people, the digital Klondike that shipped with early computers was their very first taste of card play on a screen. If you want to understand solitaire, this is the perfect place to begin.

The Goal

Your objective in Klondike is to build four foundation piles, one for each suit, in order from Ace up to King. When all four foundations are complete, you win the game. Everything you do on the board is in service of that single aim.

The Setup

The game uses a standard 52-card deck. Cards are dealt into seven tableau columns. The first column holds one card, the second holds two, and so on up to seven, with only the top card of each column face up. The remaining cards form the stock pile, which you draw from during play.

The Rules of Movement

Within the tableau, you build cards downward in alternating colors. A red six can go on a black seven, a black five on a red six, and so on. You can move a single card or an ordered group of cards between columns.

Aces move up to start the foundations, and from there each foundation builds upward by suit. When you empty a tableau column, only a King, or a sequence beginning with a King, can fill the gap. Drawing from the stock brings new cards into play whenever you run out of useful moves.

Simple Tips to Win More Often

  • Always uncover face-down cards when you can, since hidden cards are the biggest obstacle to victory.
  • Do not rush every Ace and Two to the foundations; you may still need them in the tableau.
  • Try to keep at least one column flexible so you have room to maneuver.
  • Think a couple of moves ahead rather than playing the first move you see.

These small habits make a surprising difference. Many games that feel unwinnable are actually lost to hasty moves rather than a bad shuffle, so a little patience goes a long way.
